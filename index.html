Phase 1 — First Contact

Live call script

Call every new lead within 5 minutes. Response time is your biggest variable — within 5 minutes your chances of reaching them double or triple.

Tips

  • Practice talking to them like an old friend you haven't seen in a while
  • Be personable — do anything but sound like a robot
  • The first ten seconds are so important
  • Plan for a short call, but don't be afraid to talk more if there is more to talk about
  • If you can build any kind of rapport, laugh with them, and help them feel good while talking to you, they are much more likely to be communicative in the future and want to work with you again

The script

Opener

"Hey [name], this is [rep name] — how's it going?"Let them respond. If they ask who it is:"My name is [rep] — I just got your request that you were looking for a [make/model]. Is that right?"Wait for them to confirm. Then:"Awesome, I'm with DealerKey, and we've seen several of these come through the auction just recently."

Get them talking

"Tell me a bit more about this — when are you hoping to find one by?"Listen. Follow up with one or two natural questions — year range, mileage, budget, what's driving the purchase.

Explain what you do

"Thanks for reaching out. I'm not sure if you've seen our website, but basically what we do is source cars for our clients off wholesale auctions — the same places dealerships buy from. We just charge a flat fee, and that typically saves our clients around $3,000–$4,000 compared to buying off a dealer's lot."

Schedule the IAM

"What I'll do is pull some listings for you that match exactly what you're looking for, so you can see the auction inventory for yourself and understand what I'm talking about. When's a good time tomorrow — or later this week — where we could have a quick call?"Get a specific time. Confirm before you hang up.

Common situations

How to handle the questions and pushback you'll run into before getting them to the IAM.

"What exactly is DealerKey?"⌄
"We source cars straight from Manheim — the largest wholesale auto auction in the country, where dealerships buy their inventory. We have access to the same cars and charge a flat fee instead of a markup. Most clients save $3,000–$4,000. It's a lot easier to show you than explain — I can pull up real listings on a call and you'll see exactly what I mean in about 10–15 minutes. When works for you?"
Goal is always the IAM. Phone call can substitute if they're hesitant, but only to build confidence toward a real Google Meet — you can't do the full consultation without screen share.
"Can you just send me more info?"⌄
"I could, but honestly it makes a lot more sense when I can walk you through the live data — we pull up real cars in real time and compare the prices side by side. Takes about 10–15 minutes. When are you free?"
If they still won't get on a call, send one strong listing and follow up: "That's an example — there are more. Worth a quick call to see the full picture?"
"Is this legit? How do I know this is real?"⌄
"Totally fair question — we're a licensed Utah dealer and I'm happy to send you our dealer license number so you can look us up yourself. But honestly the easiest way to see that it's real is to get on a call and look at the auction inventory together. You'll see exactly what's available and what it costs — no pitch, just data."
"I'm already talking to a dealership / found something I like"⌄
"Totally fine — what I'll do is just show you a few options to compare with what you're looking at now. The price gap is real, and it'll help you know if you're overpaying. Mind if I found a listing or two for you to look at?"

If they say yes, pull one strong listing and send it. Then: "There are a couple more like this — worth jumping on a quick call to see the full picture?"

Phase 2 — IAM + Search

Between IAM and BAM

After the IAM closes, you own the search. For the next 2–3 weeks your job is to keep the client warm, keep them informed, and surface the right car at the right moment so the BAM feels like a natural next step.

Cadence

  • Check in at least twice a week — even if there's nothing new worth sending, a quick "nothing that meets your criteria yet, still on it" keeps trust alive
  • When a new batch comes in, send the top 2–3 options. If one stands out, say so explicitly
  • Don't send everything — curate. Sending five mediocre options is worse than sending one great one
  • When you find a car worth bidding on, move fast — good inventory moves quickly

When a car is worth moving on

1
Text or call first
"Found something worth looking at for you. Sending details now." Prime them before the email lands.
2
Send the auction update email
Full details, out-the-door numbers, photos. Use the template below.
3
Follow up if no response within a few hours
Auction windows are short. A good car at a good price won't wait.
4
Schedule the BAM immediately once they show interest
Don't let momentum die between "I like this one" and the bid call.

IAM objection handling

Every objection has a scripted response. Know these cold.

"I'm not sure I'm comfortable putting money down before we've found anything."⌄
Memorize this one — comes up right after explaining the 25% bid deposit.
"That's fair, and I get it — it feels a little different than what most people are used to. The reason we do it that way is because we're putting our own money in right alongside yours. We're just as motivated as you are to get the right car at the right price. The 25% just makes sure we're both committed before we pull the trigger. And if we don't win, it comes straight back to you. You'll have all of this in writing before ANY money moves, so there's no guesswork."
"Can you walk me through how the 25% deposit actually works?"⌄
"Sure — so once we find a car you're serious about, you contribute 25% of the bid upfront and we cover the other 75%. Win the bid, you pay us the rest and we hand over the keys. Don't win, your money comes right back. Simple as that."
"I've been searching for months and haven't found anything."⌄
"I completely get that — and honestly, that's exactly the kind of situation we're here for. When you're searching on your own you're constantly trying to weigh price against mileage against condition against history, and every car is a different combination of all those variables. It's exhausting to compare objectively. That's where we really shine. First, Manheim gives us an unreal amount of data on each car — way more than anything you'd find on a public listing. And on top of that, our AI processes all of those variables simultaneously across hundreds of cars and gives you an ordered list of which options are actually saving you the most money. It takes the guesswork out completely."
"Wait — I can't test drive the car before we buy it?"⌄
"Great question, and that's exactly why Manheim is so incredibly thorough with their condition reports. Because you can't go in and test drive the cars, they are extensive with their reports. Dealerships are very particular and want to know every little detail, because their livelihood depends on it. Every vehicle is inspected by a third party, and they include absolutely everything they find. If for whatever reason we find an issue in the first few weeks that was NOT on the report, we can arbitrate and get our money back."
"What if we get the car and find something that wasn't in the report?"⌄
Memorize this one — common follow-up after the condition report explanation.
"Absolutely — I would be concerned about that too. Thankfully, every vehicle is inspected by a third party, not by Manheim itself, and they include absolutely everything they find. That limits the risk significantly, but if we do find an issue in the first few weeks that was NOT on the report, we can arbitrate and get our money back. That rarely happens, but when it has, we've been able to successfully arbitrate."
Natural moment to reinforce the warranty seed: "And beyond that arbitration window, the vehicle service contract we offer covers them for mechanical issues going forward — so they're protected on both ends."
"Isn't most of what's available at Manheim salvage or branded title?"⌄
"Almost everything we work with is clean title — that's the standard for the vast majority of what moves through Manheim. Occasionally a client specifically wants a cheap work truck and we'll look at branded titles for that, but typically for a car like what you're looking for, we'd be staying firmly in clean title territory."
"How do I know this isn't a scam?"⌄
"Totally fair question, and honestly I'd be asking the same thing if I were in your shoes. Here's the thing — what we're doing is pretty new. Manheim's wholesale auctions have been dealer-only for decades, and DealerKey is one of the first ways for regular people to actually get access to that, through a licensed dealership. So it makes sense it doesn't feel like a 'normal' car-buying experience yet, because it isn't one. That said, we're a fully licensed Utah dealer, and I'm happy to send you our dealer license number and business registration right now so you can look us up on your own time — no rush, no pressure. A lot of our clients do exactly that before moving forward, and honestly, we'd rather you feel 100% solid about it than just take our word for it."

The three closes

These happen in Section 4 of the IAM, in this order. Don't skip or reorder them.

Close 1 — Soft close
"Does email or text work better for you?"

Gets the client engaged and picturing the ongoing process. The assumption of forward motion is built in. Wait for their answer before moving on.

Close 2 — Solidifying close
"How much notice do you need before making a move? Let's say I find the perfect car and the auction runs two days from now — can you move within 48 hours?"

Gets a commitment to their readiness, not just the process. Their answer tells you exactly how serious they are. If they hesitate, address it before Close 3.

Close 3 — Strongest close
"Well [name], thanks so much for giving us a shot. So here's what we'll do — for the next 2-3 weeks, just let me handle it completely. You don't have to do a thing. If I haven't found the right one by then, no worries—we'll keep looking on our end and you can jump back in too. Sound good?"
Do not rush this. Say it, then stop talking. Their response tells you exactly how bought-in they are.
